Chief Guest
Vidya Jyothi Emeritus Professor Dayantha Wijeyesekera
Chancellor University of Vocational Technology
Sri Lanka.
Former Dean, Engineering
and former Vice Chancellor,
University of Moratuwa

 While I recall with nostalgic feelings the establishment of the ERUs in the Engineering Faculties in the Sri Lankan Universities in the 1990s, I am pleased to send a message for the MERCon 2015 booklet.

  The inclusion and showcasing of the undergraduate engineering projects is a very progressive step in the right direction.

Research, curiosity and discovery has been in existence ever since man’s presence on this planet millions of years ago, civilization has been characterized by curiosity and discovery. Therefore, the curiosity to explore what will happen, how does it happen, is there a better way to do it, has been the driving force behind all research efforts.

During the past few decades, the engineering faculties have taken a number initiatives to re- orient the university machinery to play leading roles in the industrial development process. This has further strengthened integrated multidisciplinary teams to develop a number of appropriate technologies that the traditional systems with crisp boundaries among departments have failed to produce desired outcomes.

Recognition of such outstanding research achievements among the staff is a great motivating factor while the recognition of final year engineering project achievements by academic and professional bodies are also a great encouragements for further research.

While there are number of barriers that retard the progress of such development oriented collaborative research, it is essential that academics, researchers and professionals convince the decision makers the importance of the development oriented multi-disciplinary research for the competitive advantage of nation building through institution building.

Finally while congratulating and appreciating the work done thus far to the profession and the country. Let me wish the ERU of Moratuwa every success in its endeavours


Chief Guest
Prof. J. Rohan Lucas
University of Moratuwa,
Sri Lanka
lucas@elect.mrt.ac.lk

  It gives me great pleasure to be associated with MERCon 2015, the first international conference organised by the Engineering Research Unit (ERU) of the University of Moratuwa. I am extremely glad that MERCon has emerged as a multidisciplinary international conference from its humble beginnings as the Annual Symposium on Research for Industry twenty years ago.

By 1995, the University of Moratuwa had effectively completed 25 years of producing high quality graduates, and it was high time to foster the research culture in the University, as the quality of teaching had reached high standards. An ERU was set up to encourage academic staff and students to publish their research work. Thus the fledgling ERU committee, under my Chairmanship and with Dr. Priyan Dias the General Chair of this Conference as Secretary, decided to organise annually an industry based research symposium. Presentations in the early symposia were based on a review of the abstracts only. The symposium was initially developed as a stepping stone to publishing research in peer reviewed international fora with lessons learnt.

I am happy that it has now turned a full circle, and it has become possible for the ERU to organise that sought after peer reviewed international forum with sponsorship of the internationally recognised IEEE. I wish MERCon 2015 all success. Prof Rohan Lucas Emeritus Professor of Electrical Engineering


General Chair
Prof. W.P.S. Dias
University of Moratuwa

 The English are well known for starting many great traditions, at which other nations have now surpassed them. Cricket and tennis come to mind readily. Scientific publication too has been dominated recently by the United States of America, with China now not too far behind. But predictably it is the Royal Society in London that issued the first scientific publication, namely the Proceedings of the Royal Society in 1665, and this year marks the three hundred and fiftieth anniversary of that event.

 The reasons for scientific publishing remain more or less the same now as then. Scientists are shy of publicity in the mass media, and in any case the number of people who could comprehend the recent advances in science is very small. The scientific publication is a means of communication among this privileged company of scholars. In addition, the frequency and dating of the publication enables a scientist to claim credit for priority in discovery or postulation, and thus be recognized as ‘primus inter pares’ or first among equals - scientists crave recognition from their peers rather than the public.

 Einstein, one of the few scientists in fact recognized by the public, said “My pencil is wiser that I”, by which he meant that we will only know what we want to claim as our findings when we write them down. And when we publish them, we allow the scientific community to dispute and perhaps disprove our claims. That is the way science grows.

 We must see the MERCon publications as standing in the line of this 350 year old practice. May we live up to the best traditions of that practice. Priyan Dias
